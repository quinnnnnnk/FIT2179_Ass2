Academic audit

Detailed methodology & design rationale

Each audit row includes a chart preview, source plan and concise What / Why / How rationale. Click each row to expand or collapse it.

01 Inflation–Wage Gap: dual-series line comparison

Thinking process

What: annual inflation and wage growth. Why: compare price pressure with income adjustment. How: line marks use x-position for year, y-position for annual change and colour for series.

Interaction & source

Legend filtering and hover tooltips reduce text clutter. Uses ABS Wage Price Index, ABS CPI component series and supporting essential price series.

02 Capital City Rent Ranking: sorted bar chart

Thinking process

What: capital-city CPI category movement. Why: identify where current cost pressure is highest across capital cities. How: bars compare capital cities against the selected CPI pressure measure.

Interaction & source

A dropdown lets users switch between all-groups, housing, food, transport and composite pressure measures.

03 Cost Geography: Australia proportional-symbol map

Thinking process

What: capital city coordinates and CPI pressure measures. Why: show spatial differences. How: map projection plus circle size and colour.

Interaction & source

Hover reveals exact city details without over-labelling the map. This satisfies the required geographic map component.

04 Essential Categories: before-and-after lollipop chart

Thinking process

What: monthly and annual CPI movement by category. Why: show which categories are currently creating pressure. How: paired dots compare monthly versus annual movement.

Interaction & source

The slider filters smaller increases so the reader can focus on the most important pressure points.

05 Essential Price Heatmap: intensity over time

Thinking process

What: annual CPI movement by category and city. Why: show how category pressure differs between cities. How: colour intensity encodes the index across a time-category matrix.

Interaction & source

A dropdown focuses one category at a time, keeping the matrix readable.

06 Student Budget Composition: donut chart

Thinking process

What: monthly household spending movement by category. Why: show where household spending rose in the latest month. How: arc marks show the distribution of positive spending movements.

Interaction & source

Legend highlighting and tooltips provide exact values while keeping the chart visually tidy.

07 Rent Index and Interest Rate: layered time-series

Thinking process

What: rent inflation and cash-rate movement. Why: add context to housing-related pressure. How: layered line series compare time-based trends.

Interaction & source

Interval brushing supports focused reading of specific periods. Final data should cite RBA and ABS sources.

08 City Burden Scatterplot: rent, groceries and student cost

Thinking process

What: overall CPI, housing CPI, food CPI and composite city pressure. Why: compare multiple burden dimensions at once. How: x/y position, size and colour encode different variables.

Interaction & source

The dropdown highlights one city, while hover provides exact values.

09 Monthly Inflation Snapshot: year selector line chart

Thinking process

What: monthly inflation across selected years. Why: show short-term movement that annual averages can hide. How: month is ordinal and inflation is encoded by y-position.

Interaction & source

A year dropdown keeps the chart simple while allowing comparison between years.

10 State Cost Pressure: bubble filter chart

Thinking process

What: food, transport and overall pressure scores by capital city. Why: summarise city-level stress. How: x/y position separates food and transport pressure, while size and colour show composite and overall pressure.

Interaction & source

The pressure slider filters lower-score states, making high-pressure regions easier to identify.

Data sources: charts use ABS CPI, ABS Wage Price Index, ABS Household Spending Indicator and RBA cash-rate data uploaded for this project. Source links are listed in data/data_sources.csv.
